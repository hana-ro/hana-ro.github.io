Research

Speckle Noise Reduction in Tearney Lab

Optical coherence tomography (OCT) is a non-invasive imaging method that's used to produce cross sectional images of a sample material. OCT uses a near infrared light source and based on the material properties, depth, and other characteristics of the sample, light will be scattered throughout the tissue and reflect back at different intensities to the detector of the OCT system. This creates the OCT image. One of the most important limitations of OCT imaging is speckle noise, which is caused by multiple forward and backward scattering of light waves. By integrating a heating laser, I built a bench-top fiber-optically implemented OCT system and used MATLAB to analyze and process the resulting images. This method could enhance image clarity, ultimately improving diagnostic accuracy in both clinical and research settings.

Electroadhesive Hydrogel Interface in Traverso Lab

e-Glue, an electroadhesive hydrogel interface for robust and prolonged mucosal retention, is a novel device for gastrointestinal (GI) theranostics. Following electrical activation, e-Glue can can adhere to the inside of the GI tract for up to 30 days. This device has great potential for local therapeutic delivery, advanced biosensing, and enhanced treatment and diagnostic applicationsâ€”particularly for patients with limited access to hospitals. My work was centered around the initial stages of material characterization of the device. This involved synthesizing hydrogels for tensile peeling and thermal tests. Afterwards, using MATLAB, I calculated the adhesion energy and peak tension data to analyze the material properties of various conditions being tested. This work culminated in a co-authored manuscript in Science Translational Medicine.

Description: Sway is a long-term care and nursing home bed attachment that allows nurses and caregivers to reposition mobility-impaired patients.
